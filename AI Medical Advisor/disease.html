Disease Information

Kidney, and Urinary System

Blood in Urine (Haematuria) : Blood in urine may indicate infections, stones, or tumors. Treatment depends on the underlying cause: antibiotics for infections, surgery for tumors.

Chronic Kidney Disease : Progressive loss of kidney function leading to waste build-up in the body. Managed with medications, lifestyle changes, dialysis, or kidney transplants.

Urinary Catheters : Devices used to drain urine when the bladder cannot empty naturally. Requires strict hygiene to prevent infection.

Urinary Obstruction : Blockages in the urinary tract causing pain, kidney damage, or infection. Treatments include catheterization, surgery, or medications to relieve the obstruction.

Urinary Infections (Cystitis) : Inflammation of the bladder, typically caused by bacterial infection. Symptoms include frequent, painful urination; treated with antibiotics.

Kidney Stones : Hard deposits of minerals and salts that form in the kidneys. Can cause severe pain; treatment includes pain relief, hydration, or surgery.

Kidney Infection (Pyelonephritis) : A bacterial infection of the kidneys that can cause fever, back pain, and nausea. Treated with antibiotics; untreated infections may lead to permanent kidney damage.

Bladder Control Problems (Urinary Incontinence) : Involuntary leakage of urine. Treatments include pelvic floor exercises, medications, or surgery.

Blood Disorders

Anaemia : A condition where there is a lack of healthy red blood cells, leading to fatigue and weakness. Treated with iron supplements or treatments for the underlying cause.

Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) : A blood clot that forms in a deep vein, typically in the legs. Treated with anticoagulants or surgery.

Iron Overload (Haemochromatosis) : A genetic disorder causing the body to absorb too much iron. Treated by regular blood donations or medications.

Low Iron and Iron Deficiency Anaemia : A condition caused by insufficient iron, leading to fatigue and weakness. Treated with iron supplements and dietary changes. :

Vitamin B12 Deficiency and Pernicious Anaemia : A lack of vitamin B12 can cause neurological problems and fatigue. Treated with B12 injections or supplements.

Bones and Joints

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome : A condition caused by compression of the median nerve in the wrist, leading to numbness and pain in the hand. Treated with splints, physical therapy, or surgery.

Gout : A type of arthritis caused by the buildup of uric acid crystals in joints, leading to sudden, severe pain. Treated with anti-inflammatory medications and lifestyle changes.

Rheumatoid, Psoriatic, and Inflammatory Arthritis : Autoimmune diseases that cause inflammation and damage in the joints. Treated with medications, lifestyle changes, and physical therapy.

Low Back Pain : Pain in the lower back caused by injury, aging, or structural problems. Treated with physical therapy, pain relief, and posture adjustments.

Neck Pain : Pain and stiffness in the neck due to poor posture, injury, or underlying conditions. Managed with physical therapy and pain relief.

Osteoarthritis : A degenerative joint disease that results in cartilage breakdown. Treated with pain relievers, physical therapy, and lifestyle modifications.

Osteoporosis : A condition where bones become weak and brittle. Treated with medications, calcium, and vitamin D supplements.

Brain and Nerves

Bell's Palsy : Sudden, temporary weakness or paralysis of facial muscles, typically on one side. Often improves on its own, but treatments include steroids and physical therapy.

Dementia : A general term for a decline in memory, thinking, and social abilities that interfere with daily functioning. Managed with medications and supportive care.

Cancer

Bowel Cancer : Cancer that affects the large bowel, including the colon and rectum. Treatment includes surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation.

Leukaemia : A cancer of the body's blood-forming tissues, including the bone marrow and lymphatic system. Treatments vary but typically include chemotherapy, radiation, and sometimes bone marrow transplants.

Lymphoma : Cancer of the lymphatic system, which is part of the bodyâ€™s immune system. Treatment includes chemotherapy, radiation, and sometimes stem cell transplants.

Melanoma : The most serious type of skin cancer that develops in the cells that produce melanin. Treated through surgical removal, immunotherapy, and targeted therapies.

Myeloma : A cancer of plasma cells in bone marrow that can lead to bone pain, infections, and kidney issues. Treatment includes chemotherapy, radiation, and stem cell transplants.

Non-Melanoma Skin Cancer : Less aggressive skin cancers that affect the basal and squamous cells. Typically treated with surgical excision, radiation, or topical treatments.

Ovarian Cancer : A type of cancer that begins in the ovaries and often goes undetected until it has spread. Treatment usually involves surgery and chemotherapy.

Cancer Treatments : Various treatments, including surgery, chemotherapy, radiation, and immunotherapy, are used depending on the type and stage of cancer.

Brain Tumors : Abnormal growths in or around the brain. Treatment depends on the type and location of the tumor, often involving surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy.

Breast Cancer : A type of cancer that begins in the cells of the breast. Treated with surgery, radiation, chemotherapy, and targeted therapies.

Brain and Nerves

Epilepsy : A neurological disorder marked by recurrent seizures due to abnormal electrical activity in the brain. Treatment includes anti-epileptic drugs (AEDs) or, in some cases, surgery.

Functional Neurological Disorder (FND) : A condition where patients experience neurological symptoms (e.g., seizures, weakness) without clear structural damage. Managed through physical therapy, psychological therapy, and sometimes medication.

Headache : Headaches can result from tension, migraines, or underlying health issues. Treatments include pain relievers, relaxation techniques, and sometimes prescription medication.

Head Injuries in Adults : Can range from mild concussions to severe brain trauma, often caused by falls or accidents. Treatment varies depending on severity, from rest to surgery.

Head Injuries in Children : Common in young children and can vary in severity from minor bumps to serious injuries. Treatment involves observation, rest, and medical evaluation if symptoms worsen.

Stroke : A medical emergency caused by a blockage or bleed in the brain, leading to potential brain damage. Treatments include clot-busting drugs, surgery, and rehabilitation.

Transient Ischaemic Attacks (TIA) : Often referred to as a "mini-stroke," a TIA is a temporary blockage of blood flow to the brain. Treated as a medical emergency; prevention strategies include medications and lifestyle changes.

Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes : A chronic condition where the pancreas produces little or no insulin. Managed with insulin injections, blood sugar monitoring, and lifestyle changes.

Type 2 Diabetes : A chronic condition that affects the way the body processes blood sugar. Managed through diet, exercise, medications, and sometimes insulin.

Self-Care for Diabetes : Involves monitoring blood sugar levels, diet control, exercise, and medications.

Complications of Diabetes : Includes nerve damage, kidney disease, heart disease, and vision problems. Prevented by maintaining blood sugar control and regular health check-ups.

Ear, Nose, and Throat

Colds in Adults : Caused by viral infections, leading to a runny nose, sore throat, and congestion. Treated with rest, fluids, and over-the-counter medications.

Earache : Pain in the ear caused by infections, injuries, or blockages. Treatment depends on the cause, from antibiotics for infections to pain relief.

Hearing Loss : Can result from age, exposure to loud noise, infections, or other medical conditions. Managed with hearing aids, surgery, or cochlear implants.

Sore Throat : Commonly caused by viral or bacterial infections. Treatment includes pain relievers, hydration, and antibiotics if bacterial.

Snoring : Caused by the vibration of the tissues in the throat during sleep. Treated with lifestyle changes or devices like CPAP for sleep apnea.

Sinusitis : Inflammation of the sinuses, often caused by infections or allergies. Treated with nasal sprays, antibiotics, or surgery in severe cases.

Tinnitus : A ringing or buzzing sound in the ears without an external source. Managed with sound therapy, hearing aids, or cognitive behavioral therapy.

Vertigo : A sensation of spinning, often caused by inner ear problems. Treated with medications or physical therapy exercises (e.g., Epley maneuver).

Heart Health

Angina : Chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart muscles, often a symptom of coronary artery disease. Managed through medications like nitroglycerin, lifestyle changes, and sometimes surgical procedures (e.g., stents).

Chest Pain : Can arise from various causes, including heart-related issues (e.g., angina, heart attack) or non-heart-related problems (e.g., muscle strain, acid reflux). Urgent evaluation is necessary to determine the cause.

Heart Attack : Occurs when blood flow to the heart is blocked, causing damage to heart tissue. Immediate treatment includes medications, stents, or surgery, followed by lifestyle adjustments.

Heart and Diabetes Checks : Regular checks for heart and diabetes health help to prevent complications through monitoring blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar.

Other

High Blood Pressure (Hypertension) : A chronic condition where the force of blood against artery walls is too high, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke. Managed through lifestyle changes and medications.

Liver and Gallbladder : Conditions such as gallstones, hepatitis, and liver cirrhosis affect the liver and gallbladder, leading to symptoms like jaundice, pain, and digestive issues. Treatment varies from lifestyle changes and medications to surgeries such as gallbladder removal.

Lung Diseases : Includes diseases like asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and lung infections. Treatment involves medications like inhalers, antibiotics, and lifestyle changes such as smoking cessation.
